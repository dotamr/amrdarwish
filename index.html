01 — About

Before pixels, I built worlds with paint and light.

I grew up in a family where art wasn't a hobby — it was the air we breathed. I played violin professionally (classical, Arabic, Oriental). Music taught me that precision and emotion are not opposites. They are the same thing, performed at the same time.

I studied Fine Art at Helwan University — specializing in Decoration, Theater & Film Set Design. I learned to make people feel something the moment they walk into a room. Then I built 200+ websites. Then I became the person who leads the people who build.

Different disciplines. Different decades. The same obsession: craft that moves people.

"Design is not what you add. It's what you have the courage to remove."

03 — My Process

Seven steps.
No shortcuts.

"I don't start with screens. I start with questions." Before I open Figma, I need to understand who the user is, what the business needs, and where the gap between them lives.

01
Brief
Understand what's really being asked, not just what's written down.
02
Research
Users, competitors, trends, numbers. No assumptions.
03
Wireframe
Structure before style. Logic before aesthetics.
04
Prototype
Make it real enough to test. Fail fast, learn faster.
05
Test
Real users. Real feedback. No guessing.
06
Iterate
The first solution is never the final solution.
07
Deliver
Ship with intention. Measure what matters.
